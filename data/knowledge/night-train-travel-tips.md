# Practical tips for a night-train journey

A night train is a hotel that moves, and a little preparation makes the night far better.

Pack a small overnight bag with what you need until morning, and store the large luggage on the rack, because compartments are tight and you do not want to open a big case at midnight. Couchettes have a luggage shelf above the door.

Bring water and some food. Not every night train has a catering car, and where a bar or breakfast exists it can be limited or extra. A bottle of water and a snack cover the gap.

Keep your passport, ticket, and phone on you, not in stowed luggage. The conductor usually collects tickets and sometimes passports soon after departure on cross-border routes, then returns them before your stop, so you are not woken at the frontier.

Take the bike or the car only where the train allows it. Some operators carry bicycles in a reserved space that must be booked in advance, and a few still offer motorail car transport, but most do not, so check the route before relying on it.

Bring earplugs, an eye mask, and a layer for warmth. Older couchette stock can be noisy and the temperature varies through the night.
