# Interrail and Eurail passes on night trains

An Interrail or Eurail pass covers the journey on most state night-train operators, but a sleeper or couchette still needs a separate reservation and supplement on top of the pass. The pass pays for the travel; the bed is extra.

A useful planning trick: one night train on an overnight journey counts as travel on the departure date for many pass rules, so an overnight hop can save both a hotel night and a travel day. Check the current pass conditions, since the overnight rule changes between pass versions.

Reservation supplements vary. ÖBB Nightjet charges a fixed supplement per accommodation class for pass holders. European Sleeper and Snälltåget require a paid reservation. Caledonian Sleeper and the Swedish and Norwegian night trains accept passes with a berth supplement.

Not every operator accepts passes. Private and open-access operators such as RegioJet, Leo Express, FlixTrain, Italo, Ouigo, and MTR Express do not accept Interrail or Eurail. If your route is run by one of those, you buy a normal ticket from the operator regardless of holding a pass.

Always reserve the bed early. Pass-holder berth allocations are limited and sell out before the general fares do.
