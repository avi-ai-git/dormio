# Seasons, frequency, and when to book

Night trains are not all daily. Many run on fixed weekdays, and a good number are seasonal, so the same route can exist in July and vanish in November. Always check the day of the week and the season before planning around one.

Seasonal routes cluster in summer. The Stockholm to Berlin Snälltåget, the ÖBB Nightjet links to Amsterdam and Brussels in their seasonal periods, and several Alpine and ski services run only part of the year. Summer sleepers to the coast and the mountains often sell out months ahead.

Frequency is encoded as days of the week. A route marked Monday, Wednesday, Friday leaves only on those nights. A route marked daily runs every night. Plan the return leg separately, because the two directions can run on different days.

Booking windows open two to six months before departure. ÖBB Nightjet opens about six months ahead, PKP Intercity only about thirty days ahead, and most others sit in between. The cheapest fares and the sleeper cabins are released at opening and sell first.

For festive travel, the days around Christmas and New Year are the hardest to book. Treat them like peak summer and reserve as early as the operator allows.
