# Sleeping options on a night train

Night trains sell several classes, and the comfort gap between them is large. Knowing the difference avoids booking a seat for a twelve-hour journey by mistake.

A seat is an ordinary reclining or compartment seat. It is the cheapest option and fine for a short overnight hop, but you do not lie flat, so it is tiring on a long run.

A couchette is a padded bunk in a shared compartment, usually four or six berths, with a pillow and a blanket. You sleep in your clothes and share with other travellers. Couchettes are the best value for a comfortable night and are the most common sleeper-style option.

A sleeper is a private cabin with proper beds, made up with sheets, and often a washbasin. The newer ÖBB Nightjet and the premium operators offer two or three berth sleepers, and some have an ensuite shower and toilet. This is the most comfortable and the most expensive class.

A capsule, or mini-cabin, is a newer single-person pod with a bed and a door, offered on the latest Nightjet rolling stock. It gives privacy at a price between a couchette and a full sleeper.

Women-only couchette compartments can be requested on many operators at no extra charge.
